    Implied Volatility (IV):

    Implied volatility is an estimate of the future volatility, or fluctuations, of an option's price. It only forecasts the sizes of potential price swings.

    Open Interest (OI):

    OI indicates the total number of options or futures contracts that are currently open in the market.

    Delta:

    It measures how much an option price changes for a one-point move in the underlying. Its value ranges between 0 and 1 for calls. For put side delta --- It measures how much an option price changes for a one-point move in the underlying. Its value ranges between –1 and 0 for puts

    Dummy Strike Price:

    OI indicates the total number of options or futures contracts that are currently open in the market.

    Vega:

    Vega measures the risk from changes in implied volatility. Higher volatility makes options more expensive since there is a greater chance that the underlying security price will move above or below.

    Theta:

    Theta measures the rate of time value decay and it is always a negative number as time moves in only one direction.

    Gamma:

    It measures the rate of change in the delta. Values are highest for at-the-money options and smallest for those far in or out of the money.

    ITM prob:

    It estimates the likelihood that an option will expire In-The-Money. If you want to trade conservatively with a high probability, you should find a strike price(s) that give you a high probability of profit.

    India VIX:

    The India VIX is a volatility index based on the NIFTY Index Option prices. The VIX is considered by many to be a gauge of fear and greed in the stock market moreover, the VIX may also be used as an indication of what magnitude of daily price movement is expected for the NIFTY 50.

    PCR:

    The PCR is the ratio of the open interest of the put option and the call option over a particular time. The ratio helps us identify extreme bullishness or bearishness in the market and is usually considered a contrarian indicator. Generally, a PCR value of over 1 is considered bearish and a value of less than 0.5 is considered bullish.
